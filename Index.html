Inevitability

What made a flower bloom?
Earth, sunshine, air and rain...
It would not suffice but a butterfly came,
One antenna touched the pollen.

What made we two meet?
Footsteps of coincidence and privity.
It would not suffice but the same orbit,
We have wended on already.

Autumn

Now turned aside by a steep precipice;
Now starting back from a somber chasm.
Around me are looks of doubt and malice,
While ghastly ebbs and pools open bosom.

All at once, a sudden smile of heaven,
Forth burst the sunshine, pouring a flood
Into the dark forest, gladdening each green
Leaf, transmuting the yellow ones to gold.

With a mien of calmness I behold the mixing
Of impalpable earth and unattainable west,
Though in utmost consternation the aspiring
Ambition, which vexed it with deep unrest.

But the sea, as in the old times, heaves, swells,
Foams and roars everlastingly at its own will.
